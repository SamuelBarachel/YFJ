YFJ NORTH AMERICA — OFFICIAL TRADITIONS & GOVERNANCE DOCUMENT
==============================================================
"I understood by the books..." — Daniel 9:2

SECTION 1: FOUNDATIONAL TRADITIONS
------------------------------------
1.1 The Motto
Every meeting, report, and official communication of Youth for Jesus North America begins with the sacred phrase: "I understood by the books..." This is drawn from the Book of Daniel, Chapter 9, Verse 2, and reflects our commitment to studying the Word and ordering our work by divine principles.

1.2 Opening Protocol
All official YFJ North America meetings are opened with prayer, followed by a reading of Scripture relevant to the agenda. The Meeting Chair is responsible for selecting the opening passage.

1.3 Quorum Requirements
A quorum for any official business meeting shall consist of no fewer than two-thirds (2/3) of confirmed leadership present. No binding decisions may be made without a quorum.

1.4 The Secretary's Charge
The appointed Secretary shall maintain accurate and complete records of all meetings, attendance, decisions, and correspondence. All minutes shall begin with the motto and include: date, location, members present, agenda covered, motions made, votes cast, and action items assigned.

SECTION 2: MEETING STRUCTURE
------------------------------
2.1 Order of Business
  I.   Opening Prayer
  II.  Reading of the Motto
  III. Roll Call / Attendance
  IV.  Review of Previous Minutes
  V.   Treasurer's Report (if applicable)
  VI.  Old Business
  VII. New Business
  VIII.Kingdom Activity Reports
  IX.  Announcements
  X.   Closing Prayer

2.2 Meeting Types
  - Weekly Devotional Meetings: Held as scheduled, led by the assigned Leader on the roster.
  - Monthly Coordination Meetings: Held on the last Saturday of each month for leadership coordination.
  - Quarterly Review Sessions: Held at the end of each quarter (March, June, September, December).
  - Annual General Assembly: Held every year in December, reviewing the full year's work.

2.3 Meeting Chairs
The Meeting Chair is responsible for: keeping order, managing time, ensuring the agenda is followed, and declaring the meeting closed.

SECTION 3: KINGDOM ACTIVITIES
--------------------------------
3.1 Definition
Kingdom Activities are any organized efforts by YFJ North America members to advance the mission of the organization outside of regular meetings. These include outreach events, community service, music ministry, youth programs, and evangelism campaigns.

3.2 Reporting
All Kingdom Activities must be reported at the next official meeting. Reports should include: activity name, date, location, participants, outcomes, and follow-up actions.

3.3 Approval
Kingdom Activities requiring organizational resources (funds, equipment, venue booking) must be approved by the Meeting Chair and at least one other officer at least two weeks in advance.

SECTION 4: LEADERSHIP ROLES
------------------------------
4.1 Roles & Responsibilities
  - Territory Coordinator (TC): Oversees all chapter activities within a territory. Final authority on territory decisions.
  - Regional Coordinator (RC): Coordinates between multiple chapters within a region. Reports to TC.
  - Meeting Chair: Facilitates official meetings. Appointed on a rotating or permanent basis by the TC.
  - Secretary: Responsible for all records, minutes, reports, and official communications.
  - Treasurer: Manages all funds, issues financial reports at monthly meetings.
  - Enrolled Youth (EY): Active members in good standing who participate in meetings and activities.

4.2 Leadership Ethics
All leaders are expected to: be punctual, prepared, prayerful, and professional. Conduct unbecoming of a YFJ leader may result in suspension or removal as determined by the TC.

SECTION 5: ATTENDANCE & PARTICIPATION
----------------------------------------
5.1 Attendance Records
Attendance is taken at every meeting. The Secretary records each member as: Present (P), Absent (A), or Excused (E). Three consecutive unexcused absences will trigger a pastoral follow-up.

5.2 Participation Standards
Members are encouraged to actively participate in discussions, prayer, Kingdom Activities, and service opportunities.

SECTION 6: REPORTS
---------------------
6.1 Weekly Reports
Summarize the week's meeting: attendance, devotional topic, discussions, and any assignments given.

6.2 Monthly Reports
Compile all weekly reports for the month. Include: total attendance, Kingdom Activities conducted, decisions made, and a spiritual reflection section.

6.3 Quarterly Reports
Provide a comprehensive overview of the quarter's work, including: statistical analysis of attendance, completed Kingdom Activities, financial summary, and a leadership assessment.

6.4 Yearly Reports
The Annual Report covers the full calendar year. It must include: year-in-review narrative, complete attendance statistics, all Kingdom Activities, financial report, leadership transitions, and a vision statement for the coming year.

SECTION 7: COMMUNICATIONS
----------------------------
7.1 Official Communications
All official communications from YFJ North America are to be dignified, professional, and begin with the motto where appropriate.

7.2 Announcements
Announcements at meetings should be brief (under two minutes each). Written announcements may be posted on the official communication board. Urgent announcements should be communicated to the Secretary for immediate dissemination.

7.3 Confidentiality
Discussions of a pastoral, financial, or personal nature held in executive session are strictly confidential and are not included in the general minutes.

SECTION 8: AMENDMENTS
------------------------
8.1 This document may be amended by a two-thirds majority vote of confirmed leadership at any Quarterly Review Session or Annual General Assembly.

8.2 All amendments become effective upon recording in the official minutes and notification to all members.
